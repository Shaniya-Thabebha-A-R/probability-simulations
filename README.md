# Probability Simulations 🎲

Python simulations that prove counterintuitive probability results.

1. Monty Hall Simulation

- 3 boxes, one has a laptop, two have phones.
- Host opens a phone box after your pick.
- Should you switch boxes?
- Most people say 50/50 — simulation proves switching wins 67% of the time.
- Ran 100,000 trials to verify.


2. Birthday Problem Simulation

- How many people needed before someone shares YOUR birthday?
- Most people guess around 30-40 people.
- Simulation proves you need 253 people for just a 50% chance (49.99%).
- Probability never reaches 100% — keeps climbing but never touches it.
- Ran 100,000 trials across different room sizes.

- Python.
- random module only.

## Why I built this
Both problems have answers that defy intuition.
Simulating them makes the math undeniable.
